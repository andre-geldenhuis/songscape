New Zealand North Island brown kiwi. Kiwi are active at night, and their distinctive call makes them ideal candidates for acoustic monitoring.


CC-BY Edward Abraham

Deploying a Department of Conservation sound recorder to monitor the reintroduction of North Island brown kiwi near Wainuiomata, New Zealand. The reintroduction is a community project run by the Rimutaka Forest Trust Park. Kiwi are vulnerable to mammalian predators, and ongoing monitoring is needed to ensure that the reintroduction remains sucessful.

Recording birdsong and other animal sounds is an effective technique for monitoring biodiversity. The development of portable field recorders allows for rapid collection of acoustic data. The challenge is then to manage and analyse the data.

Songscape faciltates analysis of hundreds of gigabytes of acoustic data, through a combination of automated and manual classification.

We aim to make large repositories of acoustic data publicly available, and to help get people engaged in conservation.

At this stage, songscape is more of an idea than a reality. If you would like to do something similar with your acoustic data, please get in touch.
