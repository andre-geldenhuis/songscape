New Zealand North Island brown kiwi. Kiwi are active at night, and their distinctive call makes them suited for acoustic monitoring. Photo CC-BY Edward Abraham

1. Record the noises

Audio recorders are a simple way to monitor for the presence and abundance of birds, bats, frogs, marine mammals, and many other animals. Consistent recording allows the detection of changes over time and space.

2. Analyse the data

Songscape is a service that allows researchers and community groups to manage and analyse acoustic data online. Songscape uses a combination of machine learning and human identification to identify sounds within audio files, allowing efficient processing of many hours of recordings.

3. It's about the animals

Transform audio files into data on animals of interest. Understand where populations are found, and how they are changing.

Stay in touch
